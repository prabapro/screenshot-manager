## Our Commitment to Your Privacy

**JsonKit is designed with privacy as a core principle.** The vast majority of our Service operates entirely within your browser, meaning your JSON data never leaves your device unless you explicitly choose to use features that require server interaction.

## Data Processing and Storage

### Your JSON Data

- **Client-Side Processing**: All JSON formatting, validation, minification, and manipulation occurs locally in your browser using JavaScript.
- **No Server Storage**: We do not store, cache, or have access to the JSON content you process through our Service.
- **Local File Operations**: File uploads and downloads are handled entirely by your browser without server involvement.
- **Temporary Memory**: JSON data may be temporarily held in your browser's memory during processing but is never transmitted to our servers.
- **Browser Storage**: Some preferences and recently used files metadata (not content) may be stored locally in your browser for a better user experience.

### When Data Might Leave Your Browser

In limited circumstances, data may be sent to our servers:

- **Feature Requests**: When you explicitly submit feedback or feature requests through our contact forms
- **Error Reporting**: If you choose to report bugs or errors (you control what information to include)
- **Newsletter Subscription**: If you subscribe to updates about new features

**Important**: Your actual JSON data content is never involved in these server interactions.
